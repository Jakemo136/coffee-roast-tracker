Two options: pills mixed into Notes, or a dedicated Flavors column. Scroll down to see both.

Flavor Category Colors (inspired by Sweet Maria's)

Each descriptor belongs to a category. The pill inherits its category color. Off-flavors always use red.

Design Notes

  • Flavor pills — colored dot + label, category color as tinted background. Compact enough for 3-4 per row.
  • Off-flavor pills — red with dashed border to visually distinguish from positive flavors at a glance.
  • Overflow — if a roast has 5+ flavors, show first 3 pills + "+2 more" chip.
  • Empty state — "No flavors tagged" in muted italic (row 4 in Option A).
  • 12 flavor categories mapped from Sweet Maria's palette: Floral, Honey, Sugars, Caramel, Fruits, Citrus, Berry, Cocoa, Nuts, Rustic, Spice, Body. Each individual descriptor (e.g., "Jasmine") inherits its category color ("Floral").

Which layout?

A

Separate Flavors column

7 columns: Bean | Date | Notes | Flavors | Dev Time | Dev ΔT | Rating. Cleaner separation — Notes is just your text, Flavors is just pills. Wider table.

B

Combined Notes & Flavors

6 columns: Bean | Date | Notes & Flavors | Dev Time | Dev ΔT | Rating. Text on top, pills below. More compact, taller rows. Original column count.
